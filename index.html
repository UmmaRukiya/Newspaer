10% of families with 2nd-yr middle school kids in Japan's Kanagawa Pref. in poverty: poll

YOKOHAMA -- Some 10% of families with second-year junior high school students in east Japan's Kanagawa Prefecture are in poverty, living on less than half of the median income, according to a survey conducted by the prefectural government. Gov. Yuji Kuroiwa said, "We will conduct a more detailed analysis and consider necessary measures in cooperation with the Kanagawa University of Human Services." The governor announced the results at a Feb. 19 prefectural assembly meeting in response to a question from Takashi Akano, a member of the Constitutional Democratic Party of Japan. This is the first time that the prefecture has conducted a survey on the so-called "child poverty rate." It was carried out between August and September 2023 on 4,320 randomly selected second-year junior high school students to determine the actual living conditions of the children, and also included questions for their guardians. Responses were received from 1,715 students (39.7%). The percentage of households living on less than 1,875,000 yen (about $12,700) a year, half the average annual income, was calculated as a state of relative poverty, and the result was 10.1%. In a national survey conducted by the Cabinet Office in fiscal 2021 with similar questions, the ratio was 12.9%. The prefectural government's next generation development division commented, "The percentage (in our prefecture) is below the national average, but we do not consider it a low figure by any means." Many of the parents' responses expressed a need for "support for educational expenses," while many junior high school students desired "a contact point to seek advice on higher education," "a place to stay after school" and "an opportunity to take part in sports for free." In fiscal 2024 starting this April, the prefectural government plans to expand the scope of the survey to include fifth graders and second-year high school students, as well as their guardians, to ascertain their living conditions and desired assistance in accordance with the children's age. (Japanese original by Kazuyuki Endo, Yokohama Bureau)
